I Had a Revelation

April 25th, 2019 at 1:43pm

Web development is hard and that should go without saying. This is the thought that kept repeating itself over and over last night as I was awake for hours when I thought about the things I'd have to do for this site. It's been six months since I last thought of this site and there has been something bothering me that has been a long time coming:

"I do not have a website"

Well, I guess it is only a half truth since this text is actually on a site, but when I say a website I mean a page that I am actually proud of and can show off. Every now and then I'll visit a site and go into my user settings to see if there's anything new. Every single time I do so there's a field that stares straight at me with anticipation, longing to be filled with something worthy and worthwhile: "Your Website". I have to stop in my tracks and think of what I would put there.

Over the past years I've embarked upon countless endeavors that at the time felt like the most important thing in the world. These projects were something that I had to dedicate my time to and finish, but now I can only faintly remember them. It is here that I wonder where those projects are, were they worth doing, and what do I have to show for them? Obviously I've learned a lot from those moments but I have nothing to show for it. They're all shoved off in an obscure folder somewhere on a long forgotten harddrive or been lost to time never to be seen again.

It's here that I've come to the thought that I should go and create an archive, a gallery, or a portfolio of sorts that I can be proud of and show to the world. I have plans on what I want to do, but I see a long and difficult path ahead as I try my absolute best to use all I've learned about HTML and CSS to create a site that not only serves to hold my past projects, but also represent me. I spent a bit of my morning messing in Photoshop trying to get a layout done and have learned that there is a lot that has to be taken into account and it is more difficult than one could imagine.

To end off this little post, I am thinking that I will just cobble something together that holds some of my past projects and expand on it bit by bit. It is better to have something down rather than nothing after all.
